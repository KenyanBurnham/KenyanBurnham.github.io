Information for New Students

The following is a quick guide for students who would like to use the PSPE Lab.

To return to the main menu click here.

How the Lab Works

  • The PSPE lab will operate weekdays from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM.
  • Students will reserve days and times to use the lab.
  • Lab access will be restricted and students are expected to complete all pre-requiesites before they enter the lab.
  • Unprepared and unqualified students will not be allowed in the lab. No exceptions.

How to Use the Lab At a Glance

Here's a simple at a glance view of what is required of the students:

1. Complete Safety Requirements

  • Students will need to know the rules of the lab.
  • Students will have to complete their safety certificates and email them to the lab manager.

2. Research Equipment Needed for Test

  • Students should reference the equipment list provided and decide what is necessary for their test.
  • Students should also account for resolution for certain devices and include any special requirementswhen they submit their test plan.

3. Pick a Day and Time to Conduct Test

  • Students should refer to the schedule to see available times.
  • Students who would like to reserve a lab bench for weekly use or a single use only should specify in their reservation submission.
  • Keep in mind, students are alotted a maximum of 5 hours a week.

4. Make a Reservation

  • Students will fill out the form found here.
  • The lab manager will follow up to confirm reservations.

5. Student will Conduct Test

  • Students will maintain good housekeeping during and after their lab use.
  • Students will wear appropriate closed-toed footwear and long pants.
  • Students will not bring food, tobacco, drink, cosmetics, or any consumable in the lab.
  • When students arrive they can knock on 253 to enter the lab.
  • The lab manager will let students into 252, not the students.
  • Students will inform the lab manager if a change needs to be made to their bench or equipment.
  • Not following any of the rules specified above and in the lab safety manual will result in imediate ejection from the lab. No exceptions.
