Step 1: Training





All members need to perform the following actions.

  1. Read and understand what you are responsible for in the PSPE Safety Manual.
  2. Complete the three certificates in from EH&S and submit them to kenyan.burnham@ttu.edu


PSPE Safety Manual

All lab-personnel are responsible for at least knowing these sections:

  • General Safety Policies (Page 29)
  • Minimum Required Personal Protective Equipment (Page 24)
  • Minimum Training Required (Page 21)
  • Electrocution Emergency Guidance (Page 15)
  • Emergency Contact Info (Page 5)

These are the sections that are directly important to students, knowing the rules will help you to understand how the lab manager makes decisions.

Step 2: Plan Your Test





Research your device.

Generally, you need to build a device interface board and choose what equipment you need. The following questions may be helpful when planning your test:


  • What test am I being asked to perform?
  • What configuration modes or setting does the device have internally?
  • What resolution or threshold is necessary to perform the desired test?
  • What minimum and maximum specifications does this device operate within?
  • What additional resources are required, besides testing equipment?
  • How long do I have to complete this test?
  • When will I have the time to complete this test?
